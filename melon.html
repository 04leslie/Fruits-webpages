Melon fruits

What are melon fruits?

A melon is a sweet, edible, and fleshy fruit that belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae. They can be oblong, oval, perfectly round, have rough jagged skin or smooth skin with lots of seeds enclosed in theur centers. The juicy flesh in melons can be orange, yellow, red, pink, or green.

Melons originated in the African and South-west Asian continent, from where they spread to Europe. They are included amongst the first plants to be domesticized. Melons are utilized in various forms; in immature of unripe form, they are used fresh in salads, cooked-soup, stew, curry as well as a stir-fry.

What are the benefits of melon fruits?

  • Melon seeds are also a dietary source rich in vegetable oil and proteins. Their seeds are often roasted and consumed like nuts.

  • Melons have low-calorie and low-fat content. They contain up to 90% water and are rich with essential vitamins and minerals.

  • Also, they contain antioxidants, which play a key role in protecting the body against cancer.

  • Melons help regulate Blood Pressure, boost Immune System and support Heart Health.

Some examples of melon fruits

Some examples of melon fruits include: watermelon, cantaloupe, honeydew, persian melon, casaba.
